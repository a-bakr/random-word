# Stage Academy — Communication Mastery Reference

Source: Vinh Giang's STAGE Academy. Used as reference material for coaching tips, mode prompts, and practice prompts in this app.

---

## 1. The Journey to Mastery

Four developmental stages of communication:

1. **Unconscious Incompetence** — unaware of what you don't know
2. **Conscious Incompetence** — aware of your gaps
3. **Conscious Competence** — actively working, must think about it
4. **Mastery** — second nature

---

## 2. Core Principles

- **5 Universal Laws**: voice as instrument; skills only grow with external practice; new sounds are "unfamiliar," not fake; self-judgments are temporary; be generous with energy
- **Golden Rule**: if a behavior distracts from your message, it's a problem
- **Visual vs Vocal Image**: vocal image (the moment you speak) confirms or breaks the first impression

---

## 3. The 5 Vocal Foundations (the 20/80)

| # | Foundation | What to practice |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Rate of Speech** | Vary speed. Slow down on important points. |
| 2 | **Volume** | "Lifeblood" of voice. +2–3 notches projects confidence. |
| 3 | **Pitch & Melody** | Use the full "88 keys." Avoid monotone. |
| 4 | **Tonality** | Emotion behind voice. Face is the "remote control." |
| 5 | **The Pause** | Lets the audience process. Kills "um/ah." |

---

## 4. Vocal Archetypes (4MAT system)

| Archetype | Purpose | Style | 4MAT |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Motivator** | Inspire | High energy, legato (flowing) | Why |
| **Educator** | Inform | Slower, more pauses, matter-of-fact | What |
| **Coach** | Guide | Staccato, assertive body | How |
| **Friend** | Connect | Conversational, melodic, relaxed | Open/close |

---

## 5. Storytelling

Brain chemistry triggered by story:
- **Dopamine** → focus
- **Oxytocin** → trust/bonding
- **Endorphins** → relaxation

3-step formula:

1. **Incident** — who/what/when/where + VAKS (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic, Smell)
2. **Point** — the reason for telling it
3. **Link** — why it matters to *this* listener

---

## 6. Body Language & Presence

- **Posture**: open, grounded. Wall glides for shoulders.
- **Hands**: Power Sphere. Placater (inviting) vs Leveller (authoritative).
- **Eyes**: hold until the point lands.
- **Movement**: spatial anchors for past/future, or to chunk a sequence.

---

## 7. Practical Tools

- **3-2-1 Trick**: when put on the spot, pick 3 steps / 2 types / 1 thing
- **PEP**: Point → Example → Point
- **5-Part Presentation**: Intro 12.5% / Module 1 25% / Module 2 25% / Module 3 25% / Conclusion 12.5%

## 8. STAGE Academy Module Map

| Module | Topics | Vocal Focus | Exercises |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Basics** | 5 Universal Laws, Golden Rule, Visual vs Vocal Image | Vocal image | Try new vocal image in low-risk setting (ordering coffee) |
| **2. Vocal Foundations** | Rate, Volume, Pitch/Melody, Tonality, Pause | Pitch, pace, pause, power, melody | Siren technique; read kids' books with exaggerated emotion; record & review |
| **3. Vocal Archetypes** | Motivator, Educator, Coach, Friend, 4MAT | Vocal variety | Count or recite ABCs while switching archetypes |
| **4. Storytelling** | 3 Cs, Incident/Point/Link, brain chemicals | Emotional resonance | Fax Roll Strategy; 15-second "trailer" stories |
| **5. Body Language** | Posture, hand gestures (Satir), eye contact, anchoring | Physical presence | Wall glide; toe/heel grounding; "catch their eye" walk |

## Notes for app integration

Modes likely to map cleanly to the existing recording flow:

- **Pace** — annotate words with slow-down marks
- **Volume** — annotate with soft/LOUD dynamics
- **Pitch / Melody** — wave underline showing rise/fall
- **Pause** — slash marks between phrases
- **Tonality** — emotion prompts (say it warm / firm / curious)
- **Archetype** — read the prompt as Motivator / Educator / Coach / Friend
